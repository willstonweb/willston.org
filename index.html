Learn web development at the Willston Center summer 2022!

We're teaching web development to high schoolers and giving away computers for students who have demonstrated a desire to learn.


Details

Who?

We are looking for high schoolers who:

  • Live near the Willston Multicultural Center
  • Are interested in web development
  • Do not have access to a personal computer

What?

In partnership with Comunidad, Willston Web will host a bootcamp to teach web development. Students will:

  • Learn HTML and CSS fundamentals
  • Use version control (git)
  • Create a publicly accessible website

When?

The Willston Web bootcamp will be held over summer 2022 – dates TBD. Sustained mentorship for select students will continue throughout the 2022-2023 school year.

Where?

The Willston Web bootcamp will be held at the Willston Multicultural Center:

6131 Willston Drive Falls Church, VA, 22044

Why?

This program was created to solve a demonstrated need when Ricardo asked Jonathan to teach him how to do web development.

Learn more about our story!

How do I get a computer?

The number of computers we will be able to give away will depend on funding, so it is possible that not every eligible student will receive one. Laptops will be prioritized for students who meet all three of the following criteria:

  • Complete our curriculum
  • Do not own a computer
  • Show promise/potential in web development and programming

Our story


"Can you teach me?"
– Ricardo Perez, co-founder and inspiration for Willston Web

Willston Web was born of friendship: when expressing his frustration with the monotony of his current job sorting clothes in retail, Ricardo asked Jonathan, "Can you teach me [web development]?" upon hearing about Jonathan's work managing websites. Jonathan had been wondering how he could make web development more accessible and share skills he had developed at his science and technology focused high school, so he was elated to hear Ricardo desired to learn web development and thought that there might be others like him. Together, they are planning the Willston Web bootcamp. Along with increasing access to personal computers and inspiring students to pursue a career in programming, they want to foster a web of students that learn together in a community effort.
